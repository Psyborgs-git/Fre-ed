## What is a candlestick?

A **candlestick** is a single unit of price information that packs four values — Open, High, Low, and Close — into one visual symbol. Developed by Japanese rice traders in the 18th century, candlestick charts remain the most widely used price chart in professional trading.

Each candle tells a story:

| Part | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Body** (wide rectangle) | Distance between open and close |
| **Upper wick** (thin line above) | Highest price reached during the period |
| **Lower wick** (thin line below) | Lowest price reached during the period |
| **Green / filled body** | Close **above** open — buyers won the period |
| **Red / hollow body** | Close **below** open — sellers won the period |

## OHLC data structure

Every candlestick is four numbers. In Python, OHLC data usually arrives as a DataFrame:

<CodeBlock language="python">{`import pandas as pd

# Typical OHLC DataFrame from a broker or exchange API
df = pd.DataFrame({
    'timestamp': pd.date_range('2024-01-01', periods=5, freq='1D'),
    'open':  [100.0, 106.0, 108.0, 107.0, 104.0],
    'high':  [108.0, 110.0, 112.0, 111.0, 109.0],
    'low':   [ 98.0, 104.0, 105.0, 103.0, 101.0],
    'close': [106.0, 108.0, 107.0, 104.0, 108.0],
    'volume':[42000, 55000, 38000, 60000, 47000],
})

# Classify each candle
df['bullish'] = df['close'] > df['open']
df['body_size'] = (df['close'] - df['open']).abs()
df['upper_wick'] = df['high'] - df[['open', 'close']].max(axis=1)
df['lower_wick'] = df[['open', 'close']].min(axis=1) - df['low']

print(df[['timestamp', 'open', 'high', 'low', 'close', 'bullish', 'body_size']])
`}</CodeBlock>

The **body size** tells you the conviction of the move; the **wick length** tells you how far price explored before reversing.

## Body and wicks — the anatomy

$$
\text{Body} = |C - O|
$$

$$
\text{Upper wick} = H - \max(O, C)
$$

$$
\text{Lower wick} = \min(O, C) - L
$$

A **long upper wick** on a green candle means buyers pushed price high, but sellers stepped in and pushed it back before the close — a sign of overhead resistance. A **long lower wick** on a red candle shows sellers tried to push price down but failed — demand absorbed the selling.

<Callout type="tip">
**Wick-to-body ratio** is a quick way to measure indecision. A ratio above 2× often signals a potential reversal — the market probed a level and rejected it forcefully.
</Callout>

## Bullish patterns

| Pattern | Description | Signal |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| **Hammer** | Small body, long lower wick, at support | Bullish reversal |
| **Bullish Engulfing** | Large green candle fully engulfs prior red candle | Strong bullish reversal |
| **Morning Star** | Red candle → small indecision → large green candle | Bullish reversal after downtrend |
| **Marubozu** | Full body, no wicks | Overwhelming buyer control |

<CodeBlock language="python">{`def is_hammer(row, wick_ratio=2.0):
    """Identify hammer candles: small body, long lower wick."""
    body = abs(row['close'] - row['open'])
    lower_wick = min(row['open'], row['close']) - row['low']
    upper_wick = row['high'] - max(row['open'], row['close'])
    if body == 0:
        return False
    return lower_wick >= wick_ratio * body and upper_wick <= 0.2 * body

def is_bullish_engulfing(prev, curr):
    """Prior candle is bearish, current candle is bullish and larger."""
    prev_bearish = prev['close'] < prev['open']
    curr_bullish = curr['close'] > curr['open']
    engulfs = curr['open'] <= prev['close'] and curr['close'] >= prev['open']
    return prev_bearish and curr_bullish and engulfs

df['hammer'] = df.apply(is_hammer, axis=1)
# Engulfing needs pairs
df['bullish_engulf'] = [
    False if i == 0 else is_bullish_engulfing(df.iloc[i-1], df.iloc[i])
    for i in range(len(df))
]
`}</CodeBlock>

## Bearish patterns

| Pattern | Description | Signal |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| **Shooting Star** | Small body, long upper wick, at resistance | Bearish reversal |
| **Bearish Engulfing** | Large red candle fully engulfs prior green candle | Strong bearish reversal |
| **Evening Star** | Green candle → small indecision → large red candle | Bearish reversal after uptrend |
| **Dark Cloud Cover** | Red candle opens above prior close, closes below midpoint | Bearish momentum shift |

<Callout type="warning">
Candlestick patterns work best when combined with **volume confirmation** and **context** (e.g., is the hammer at a key support level?). A hammer in the middle of a range is far less meaningful than one at a 52-week low.
</Callout>

## How market makers read candles

Professional traders and market makers watch candles for **order flow imbalance**. A large green candle with high volume signals aggressive buyers; a large red candle with high volume signals aggressive sellers. Small bodies with long wicks at key levels often indicate **stop hunts** — brief excursions beyond support or resistance designed to trigger retail stop-loss orders before reversing.

The volume bar beneath each candle is equally important:

$$
\text{Dollar Volume} = \frac{O + H + L + C}{4} \times V
$$

High volume on a reversal candle confirms the move is institutionally backed. Low volume on a breakout candle is a red flag — the move may lack follow-through.
